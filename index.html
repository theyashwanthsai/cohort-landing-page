Sound familiar

You're not behind on AI. You're behind on proof.

You've watched the demos. You've opened ChatGPT, typed something, got a mediocre answer, and closed it. Meanwhile:

Your manager asked "how are you using AI in your work?" and you didn't have a real answer.

The honest part

AI can do parts of your job. It can't do the parts that took you 10 years.

In week 0 we map this for your exact role. For a typical mid-career, non-coding role, it looks like this:

AI CAN DO THIS TODAY
  • Writing draftsTest cases, reports, documentation, summaries. AI writes these in seconds.
  • Sorting and routingDeciding which ticket, defect, or request goes to whom. AI sorts faster than any person.
  • Rule-based checksAny check that follows a written rule. This is the first thing companies automate.
AI CAN'T DO THIS
  • Know your domainYour industry's rules, your client's systems, how things actually work at your company. You know this. AI doesn't.
  • Tell right from wrongAI produces answers fast, but it can't tell which ones are wrong. Someone with experience has to check.
  • Make the judgment callsWho to call, when to escalate, when to push back. That took you 10 years to learn.

So the plan is simple: you use AI to do the left column, and your experience to check its work. People who can do both are the ones companies keep. Learning to do both is exactly what this sprint is.

I haven't coded in 10+ years (or ever). Can I do this?

Yes. That's the design assumption, not an edge case. The builds use AI-assisted coding and simple scripting; what matters is your domain judgment for validating what the AI produces. If you once wrote code long ago, that dormant background helps, but it isn't required.

Why do I have to post on LinkedIn?

Because the visible, dated record is half the outcome. The posts are your proof-of-work for recruiters, managers, and your own network, and the accountability keeps you finishing. We help you draft every post.

Will my company data be involved?

No. Everything you build uses anonymized or synthetic versions of the kind of material you work with. We cover how to anonymize safely in Week 0.

What if the timings don't work for me?

Live sessions are recorded, and most of the sprint is async with group support. But if you can't make most live sessions, wait for a future cohort. The live cohort energy is a real part of why people finish.

Why only 15 seats?

Because every member gets a personal audit and projects chosen for their specific job. That doesn't scale past a small group, and we'd rather 15 people finish with real proof than 100 people watch videos.
