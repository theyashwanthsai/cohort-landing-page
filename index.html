The honest part

Some of your work is exposed. Most of your value isn't.

Every cohort member starts with a personal audit — the same analysis we run for 1:1 clients. Here's what it typically finds for a mid-career, non-coding role:

EXPOSED — AI is coming for this
  • First-draft production workTest cases, documentation, reports, summaries — generative AI drafts these in seconds.
  • Classification & routingTriaging tickets, defects, requests — LLMs pre-sort faster than any human queue.
  • Repetitive validationChecks that follow a written rule are exactly what AI automation eats first.
DEFENSIBLE — your actual moat
  • Domain & regulatory depthAI has no institutional context. Someone still has to supply it and validate against it.
  • Judgment & escalationKnowing what matters, who to call, and when to push back — organizational trust, not generation.
  • Tacit system knowledgeA decade of client-specific knowledge that was never written down anywhere an AI could learn it.

The move isn't to compete with AI on the exposed side. It's to put AI to work on the exposed side yourself — supervised by your moat. That combination is what this sprint builds, with your own work as the raw material.

Fit check

Built for a specific person. Maybe you.

THIS IS FOR YOU IF

  • 10+ years of experience in a non-coding role — QA, BA, project/delivery, scrum, ops, support, data
  • Zero or near-zero hands-on AI exposure
  • You can commit ~1–1.5 hrs on weekdays alongside a full-time job
  • You're willing to post your progress publicly on LinkedIn
  • You want proof you can show, not another certificate

THIS IS NOT FOR YOU IF

  • You're a developer who can already build with AI — you need depth, not this
  • You have under ~5 years of experience — there's no moat to build on yet

I haven't coded in 10+ years (or ever). Can I do this?

Yes — that's the design assumption, not an edge case. The builds use AI-assisted coding and simple scripting; what matters is your domain judgment for validating what the AI produces. If you once wrote code long ago, that dormant background helps, but it isn't required.

Why do I have to post on LinkedIn?

Because the visible, dated record is half the outcome. The posts are your proof-of-work for recruiters, managers, and your own network — and the accountability keeps you finishing. We help you draft every post.

Will my company data be involved?

No. Everything you build uses anonymized or synthetic versions of the kind of material you work with. We cover how to anonymize safely in Week 0.

What if the timings don't work for me?

Live sessions are recorded, and most of the sprint is async with group support. But if you can't make most live sessions, wait for a future cohort — the live cohort energy is a real part of why people finish.

Why only 15 seats?

Because every member gets a personal audit and personally-chosen capstones. That doesn't scale past a small group — and we'd rather 15 people finish with real proof than 100 people watch videos.
